Why a simple CV isn’t enough

A CV lists facts; decision-makers skim for a thesis. Without structure and interpretation, key signals are lost.

  • Department pages → stale & fragmented. Hard to update, inconsistent formatting, buried links; credibility erodes.
  • DIY templates → generic outputs. Tools provide blocks, not argument; the result is style without synthesis.
  • CV dumps → high friction. Walls of text, no hierarchy; readers cannot infer impact or through-line.
  • Consequences → missed opportunities. Fewer inquiries from students, collaborators, funders; weaker first impression.

A website is a scholarly argument

Academics persuade with ideas, not decoration. Your website should function like a paper: it advances a thesis, supports it with evidence, and leads readers through a deliberate structure.

Thesis

State clearly why your research matters and what perspective you bring.

Evidence

Show the publications, grants, collaborations, and teaching that support your claim.

Structure

Arrange content to guide readers logically toward trust and engagement.

When thesis, evidence, and structure align, your website becomes a persuasive digital identity—not a static CV.
